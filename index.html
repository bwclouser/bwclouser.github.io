Benjamin Clouser, Ph.D.

I am an atmospheric scientist whose research focuses on using water vapor isotopologues to better understand our atmosphere.

Water Isotopologues

The isotopic ratio of water yields insight into the microphysics of ice crystal growth and the convective history of an air parcel.

Cold Cirrus Microphysics

The microphysics of cirrus growth constrain the humidity of air entering the stratosphere, which has important effects on stratospheric chemistry and Earth's radiation budget. Data taken at the AIDA cloud chamber

Instrument Development

Near Real Time Concentrations

I am currently working on a project which aims to use convolutional neural networks to make rapid predictions of water vapor concentrations airborne samples. The model is trained on raw spectra and fitted concentrations from the ACCLIP campaign.

Interpreting Water Vapor Isotopologues with Backtrajectory Models

To properly interpret isotopic data taken during the ACCLIP campaign, I am developing a convective influence product. Backtrajectories from a given flight path a calculated using TRACZILLA and interaction with convection is assesed using cloud top heights derived from the GridRad radar product. The software related to this project is written in IDL and C++ and can be found here.

Satellite Retrievals of Water Isotopologues

The ACE-FTS experiment provides satellite measurements of water vapor isotopologues in Earth's atmosphere. I have developed software to process and visualize these data. This software was used to extract the water isotopic conecntrations found in Khaykin, et al., 2022. The software can be found here.

Innovation

During the development of the ChiWIS-airborne instrument I invented a non-axially-symmetric optical component which increased the instrument's signal-to-noise ratio by a factor of 3, which is described in Clouser, et al., 2018. The component was patented by the University of Chicago, and I have explored its commercial potential through the NSF I-Corps Program.

Optical Simulation Software

I developed precise geometrical ray tracing software used to design the ChiWIS-airborne instrument.

Spectral Simulation Software

I developed software for generating artifical spectra using parameter from the HITRAN database. This software was used to find optimal spectral features from which to extract concentrations for the ChiWIS-airborne instrument.

Chicago Transit Project

I developed software to analyze Chicago Ride Sharing and Taxi data.

Data Processing Software

I have developed software to reduce our field data (~50 Gb per flight) to an amount that can be fit in a short amount of time. The softare utilizes multithreading to process the data as fast as possible.
